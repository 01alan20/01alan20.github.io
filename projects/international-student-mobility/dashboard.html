Key Insights

🌏 Major Mobility Hubs

The United States, United Kingdom, and Australia attract the largest numbers of international students, with China, India, and other Asian countries as primary sources.

📉 COVID-19 Disruption

Student flows dropped significantly in 2020-2021 due to travel restrictions and campus closures. Partial recovery visible by 2023, but not yet at pre-pandemic levels globally.

🌐 Regional Patterns

Asian students (especially from China and India) comprise majority of international students in Western universities. Intra-regional mobility in Africa and Latin America remains lower.

💡 Emerging Trends

Growing flows from Southeast Asia and Middle East. Some evidence of "alternative pathway" destinations (Canada, Singapore, Australia) gaining share as competition intensifies.

Data & Methods

Data Source: "Resilient but Uneven: International Student Mobility Reconfiguration Through COVID-19" research dataset covering 2015-2023 flows from origin to destination countries.

Arrow Sizing: Flow arrow thickness represents the volume of students flowing from one country to another. Larger arrows = more students.

Note: This dashboard shows flows as reported. Some flows may be zero due to data collection limitations or countries not actively tracking international student origin at fine levels of detail.
