# Preparing to Compete Closing Design

## Objective

Reframe the project title and closing passage so the story ends with the practical ways institutions may compete as the traditional student pool contracts.

## Approved title

Use `The Enrollment Squeeze: Preparing to Compete` as the page title and primary hero heading. Preserve `The Enrollment Squeeze` as the concise project identity in the navigation and footer.

## Approved closing section

Replace the current ending with the heading:

`Institutions will compete on brand, value, outcomes, and student experience`

The closing copy will explain that:

- institutional brand is built from value, graduate outcomes, price, and student experience;
- career preparation and employment outcomes can strengthen reputation and future admissions;
- institutions may need to recruit beyond their traditional geographic and demographic reach, both domestically and internationally;
- students and families will continue evaluating price against outcomes;
- student support, career services, and work-based learning affect retention, completion, and career readiness;
- growing urban areas may offer access to people, employers, internships, and professional networks, but location alone does not produce strong outcomes; and
- future demand will depend on connecting price, quality, support, and career outcomes.

## Placement and presentation

Place this section at the bottom of the main story, immediately before the footer. Reuse the existing ending-section visual treatment so the change does not introduce a new component or alter the page's design system.

## Writing constraints

- Do not use em dashes.
- Present the claims as competitive considerations, not guaranteed causal outcomes.
- Keep the tone concise, analytical, and consistent with the rest of the project.
