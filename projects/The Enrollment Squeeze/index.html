The analytical question

How much adaptation would it take to keep enrollment level?

The demographic decline is visible. The harder questions are how far participation must rise, which institutions are already outperforming their markets, and how many students or tuition dollars must be replaced.

The conclusion

The pool sets the constraint. Competition and revenue models determine the consequence.

The squeeze is unlikely to affect every college equally. Stronger institutions may preserve enrollment by gaining market share, while institutions already losing students and dependent on shrinking markets may face greater pressure.

Sustained enrollment and revenue weakness can contribute to program reductions, staffing cuts, consolidation, merger or closure, but this analysis does not predict those outcomes for individual institutions.
