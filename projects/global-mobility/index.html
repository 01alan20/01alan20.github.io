        Read the evidence carefully

        Method, limits and reproducibility

        01

        Global totals

        Published historical UNESCO/DAAD totals are used from 2014 onward. The 2013 value is a transparent interpolation between revised 2012 and 2014 totals. The bilateral headline-route archive used in this project begins in 2016.

        02

        Global corridors

        The route layer contains selected major flows transcribed from annual Wissenschaft weltoffen maps from 2016 onward. The source figures generally show corridors of approximately 15,000 students or more; this is not a complete bilateral matrix.

        03

        Definitions vary

        Origin is ideally based on prior education, then usual residence; citizenship may be used where better measures are unavailable. Country notes and series breaks matter.

        04

        European network

        The Erasmus+ section uses cumulative Key Action 1 learning-mobility country flows since 2014, through the 2024 release. It is a different measure from global degree mobility.

        Methodology note

        Two maps. Two measures.

        Global, 2013–2023

        Internationally mobile tertiary students are people who physically crossed a national border to study. The preferred origin measure is prior education, followed by residence; citizenship is a fallback. The route layer is a curated set of published major corridors, not a full matrix.

        Europe, 2014–2024 cumulative

        The European section uses Erasmus+ Key Action 1 learning-mobility flows. It counts cumulative participant movements between sending and receiving countries. It must not be read as a continuation of the global degree-mobility series.

        Calculated measures

        Growth rates, balances, concentration and diversity measures are calculated within this project. They are analytical outputs—not official rankings from UNESCO, DAAD or the European Commission.

        Files

        All processed CSV files, source notes and Python build scripts are included in the repository folder for inspection and reuse.
